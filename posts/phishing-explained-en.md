## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering technique used by cybercriminals to deceive individuals and obtain confidential information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, or banking information. Attackers typically impersonate trusted entities like banks, well-known companies, or even coworkers through emails, instant messages, or fake websites.

## Common Types of Phishing

There are several forms of phishing, including:

*   **Spear Phishing:** Targeted attacks on specific individuals or organizations, using personal information to make the fraud more convincing.
*   **Whaling:** A type of spear phishing specifically targeting high-level executives or important figures within an organization.
*   **Smishing:** Phishing carried out through SMS (text messages).
*   **Vishing:** Phishing carried out through phone calls (voice phishing).
*   **Clone Phishing:** Creating an email nearly identical to a legitimate one the victim may have previously received, but with malicious links or attachments.

## How to Identify a Phishing Attack

Watch for these signs:

1.  **Suspicious Sender:** Check the sender's email address. Often, it is similar to the legitimate one but with small changes.
2.  **Generic Greetings:** Phishing emails frequently use impersonal greetings like "Dear customer."
3.  **Sense of Urgency:** Messages creating a sense of urgency, threatening to block accounts or charge fees if you don't act immediately.
4.  **Grammar and Spelling Errors:** Many phishing emails contain obvious errors.
5.  **Suspicious Links:** Hover over links (without clicking!) to see the actual URL. Be suspicious if it looks strange or doesn't match the expected site.
6.  **Requests for Personal Information:** Legitimate companies rarely ask for confidential information via email.
7.  **Unexpected Attachments:** Don't open attachments from unknown or unexpected senders.

## How to Protect Yourself

*   **Be Suspicious:** Be skeptical about unsolicited emails and messages.
*   **Verify Links:** Before clicking, verify the authenticity of links.
*   **Don't Provide Data:** Never provide personal or financial information in response to an email or click on a suspicious link.
*   **Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Enable MFA whenever possible to add an extra layer of security.
*   **Keep Software Updated:** Update your operating system, browser, and antivirus software.
*   **Educate Yourself:** Stay informed about the latest phishing tactics.

Remember, vigilance is your best defense against phishing.
