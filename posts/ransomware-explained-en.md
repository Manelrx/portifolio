## What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software (malware) that encrypts the victim's files or locks access to their system, demanding a ransom payment to restore access. It has become one of the most lucrative and damaging cyber threats for individuals and organizations worldwide.

## How Does It Work?

The attack typically follows these steps:

1.  **Infection:** The ransomware enters the system through various vectors, such as phishing emails with malicious attachments, links to compromised websites, exploitation of software vulnerabilities, or even through compromised networks.
2.  **Execution:** Once in the system, the ransomware executes, often silently in the background.
3.  **Encryption/Lock:** The malware begins encrypting important files (documents, photos, databases) or locks access to the operating system.
4.  **Ransom Note:** A message is displayed to the victim informing them of the attack, payment instructions (usually in cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin), and frequently a deadline for payment, after which data may be permanently lost or the ransom amount increased.

## Common Types of Ransomware

*   **Crypto Ransomware:** Encrypts the victim's files (e.g., WannaCry, CryptoLocker, Ryuk).
*   **Locker Ransomware:** Locks access to the operating system, preventing device use (e.g., Reveton).
*   **Ransomware as a Service (RaaS):** Platforms where ransomware developers sell or lease their tools to other cybercriminals, facilitating the spread of attacks.

## How to Prevent It

Prevention is the best strategy against ransomware:

*   **Regular Backups:** Keep updated, secure backups of your important data. Ideally, follow the 3-2-1 rule (three copies, on two different types of media, with one copy offline/off-site).
*   **Be Careful with Emails and Links:** Be extremely cautious with attachments and links in emails, especially from unknown senders.
*   **Keep Everything Updated:** Apply security patches and updates to your operating system, browser, antivirus, and other software regularly.
*   **Use Security Software:** Install and keep updated a good antivirus and anti-malware software with specific ransomware protection.
*   **Principle of Least Privilege:** Grant users only the permissions necessary to perform their tasks.
*   **Network Segmentation:** Isolate critical systems to limit the spread of a potential infection.
*   **Education and Awareness:** Train users to recognize phishing attempts and other social engineering tactics.

## What to Do If Infected?

1.  **Isolate the System:** Immediately disconnect the infected computer from the network to prevent the ransomware from spreading.
2.  **Don't Pay the Ransom (Recommended):** Paying doesn't guarantee data recovery and funds criminal activities. Seek professional help.
3.  **Identify the Ransomware:** Use tools like ID Ransomware (Crypto Hunter) to try to identify the variant.
4.  **Look for Decryptors:** Check if decryption tools are available for the specific variant (e.g., No More Ransom Project).
5.  **Restore from Backup:** If you have secure backups, format the infected system and restore your data.
6.  **Report the Incident:** Inform the relevant authorities.

Ransomware is a serious threat, but with good security practices and preparation, it is possible to significantly mitigate the risks.
